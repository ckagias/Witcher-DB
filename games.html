The games

CD Projekt Red's trilogy brought Sapkowski's Continent to life in a way no adaptation had managed before. Below are the three mainline entries plus the upcoming fourth chapter.

The Witcher

2007 RPG CD Projekt Red ★ 85 Metacritic

The game that started everything. Geralt wakes up at Kaer Morhen with no memory — the classic amnesia setup that lets newcomers learn the world alongside the protagonist. What it lacked in technical polish it made up for in atmosphere: grimy taverns, moral ambiguity in every quest, and a combat system that rewarded patience over button mashing. The Enhanced Edition, released a year later, fixed most of the rough edges and remains the definitive version.

Official trailer

Enhanced Edition (2008) — A free upgrade for all owners, it added five hours of new content, a complete dialogue re-record, and significant performance improvements. If you're playing today, this is the version to grab.

The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings

2011 RPG CD Projekt Red ★ 88 Metacritic

A sharp step up in every direction. The REDengine debut delivered visuals that embarrassed its console-generation peers, and the story structure leaned hard into the idea that your choices actually split the experience in two. Depending on who Geralt sides with in Chapter 1, you play through a completely different Chapter 2. It's shorter than its successor but tighter — every quest feels deliberate, and the political intrigue between Temeria, the Nilfgaardian threat, and the Scoia'tael holds up remarkably well.

Official trailer

Enhanced Edition (2012) — Brought the game to Xbox 360 and added several hours of new quests, an Arena mode, and the "Fallen Knights" side story. PC owners received it free.

PC PS4 / PS5 Xbox One / Series Switch

  • Completionist 200+ hours
  • Setting Velen, Novigrad, Skellige, Toussaint

The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt

2015 Open World RPG CD Projekt Red ★ 92 Metacritic

The definitive Witcher experience and, for many, the benchmark for open-world RPGs. Geralt's search for Ciri spans three distinct regions — the war-torn plains of Velen, the merchant city of Novigrad, and the windswept archipelago of Skellige — each with its own politics, ecology, and tone. The writing is the real achievement: side quests here have more narrative craft than most games' entire campaigns. Two major expansions followed, with Hearts of Stone widely regarded as some of the best story DLC ever made.

Official cinematic trailer

Next-Gen Update (2022) — A free upgrade for PC, PS5, and Xbox Series added ray tracing, faster loading, reworked textures, and a handful of new quests and items inspired by the Netflix series. The Complete Edition bundles both expansions and all free DLC.

The Witcher 4

TBA Open World RPG CD Projekt Red Unreal Engine 5

A new chapter, a new protagonist. Ciri takes the lead for the first time in a mainline entry, stepping into a world that has moved on from the events of the trilogy. The cinematic reveal shown at The Game Awards 2024 confirmed the premise without giving much away — which, given CDPR's track record, is probably the right call. The game marks the studio's transition to Unreal Engine 5 and the beginning of a new saga set in the same Continent, with familiar lore but fresh stakes.

Cinematic reveal — The Game Awards 2024

Witcher Schools

Each school forges its witchers differently — with its own methods, creed, and reputation. Select a school to view its profile, or use the comparison table to jump between them.

School of the Wolf

The most well-known and widely respected witcher school.

Kaer Morhen sits in the Kaedweni mountains, and the Wolf school grew out of it. Geralt, Eskel, Lambert and Vesemir all trained there. Wolf witchers don't lean hard into any single discipline — they use Signs, potions and the sword in roughly equal measure, which is why they're the most recognisable face of the profession. The keep was sacked centuries ago and was never rebuilt to full strength; the brothers who remain treat it more like a winter home than a fortress.

Stronghold Kaer Morhen, Kaedwen
Combat style Balanced — sword & Signs
Reputation Neutral, professional
Status Near extinct — few survivors

Known witchers

Geralt of Rivia Vesemir Eskel Lambert Coen

School of the Cat

Fast, ruthless and morally ambiguous.

Cat witchers have a bad name, and most of it is earned. They move fast, hit hard, and wear as little armour as they can get away with. Unlike Wolf witchers, they'll take contracts on humans if the coin is right — a habit that makes other schools keep their distance. Their base at Var Attre fell into ruin long ago, and what remains of the school is scattered across the Continent.

Stronghold Var Attre (ruins)
Combat style Fast, aggressive, light armour
Reputation Feared, untrustworthy
Status Largely scattered

Known witchers

Gaetan Brehen Kiyan

School of the Bear

Heavy, enduring and built for survival.

The Bear school trains for punishment. Its witchers wear the heaviest armour of any school and fight in a slow, grinding style built around outlasting whatever they're up against. They operate mostly in the cold north and east — territory where monsters tend to be big, brutal, and resistant to anything subtle. Haern Caduch, their stronghold in the Amell Mountains, is one of the few witcher keeps still in active use.

Stronghold Haern Caduch, Amell Mountains
Combat style Heavy armour, slow power strikes
Reputation Reliable, stoic
Status Active but small in number

Known witchers

Gerd Louis Merse

School of the Griffin

Sign-focused, scholarly and principled.

Griffin witchers treat Signs as a core weapon, not a last resort. Founded by Erland of Larvik in Kovir, the school developed a more scholarly tradition than most — its witchers tend to think before they swing, and they're known for holding to a code that other schools find impractical. Whether that's admirable or naïve depends who you ask.

Stronghold Kaer Seren, Kovir
Combat style Sign-heavy, medium armour
Reputation Principled, scholarly
Status Active

Known witchers

Erland of Larvik (founder) Coën

School of the Viper

Secretive, poison-specialised and shrouded in mystery.

Nobody knows much about the Viper school, and that's by design. Its witchers are specialists in poison and close-quarters killing — methods that blur the line between monster hunter and assassin. Their keep's location is unknown; it may not exist anymore. Letho of Gulet, the Kingslayer, is the only member most people have heard of, and he came very close to bringing down the Northern Kingdoms.

Stronghold Unknown (possibly destroyed)
Combat style Poisons, deception, raw strength
Reputation Dangerous, secretive
Status Near extinct — possibly one survivor

Known witchers

Letho of Gulet Auckes Serrit

School of the Manticore

Desert guardians — Zerrikanian “alqatil” bound to the Korath.

When Iwan broke from the group that would become the Viper school, he led his followers south to the edge of the Korath desert. Zerrikania gave them two keeps — Behelt Nar and Bialsuf Alsarea — in exchange for escorting royal caravans through the sands. Manticore witchers built their craft around endurance and preparation: heavy potion use, shields of meteoritic steel and silver, and a defensive style suited to the desert's demands. The Day of Fire in 1146 wiped out most of the order along with much of the Zerrikanian court. The few survivors wintered at Behelt Nar and kept taking contracts, but the school never recovered its numbers.

Stronghold Behelt Nar & Bialsuf Alsarea (Korath desert)
Combat style Potions, shields, caravan defence
Reputation Mysterious to Nordlings; elite escorts
Status Decimated — handful of survivors

Known witchers

Iwan (founder, later Imad Asem) Merten Sorensen

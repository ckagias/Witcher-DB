Witcher Schools

Each school forges its witchers differently — with its own methods, creed, and reputation. Select a school to view its profile, or use the comparison table to jump between them.

School of the Wolf

The most well-known and widely respected witcher school.

Based at Kaer Morhen in Kaedwen, the Wolf school is the origin of Geralt of Rivia and most of the witchers encountered across the games. Known for balanced combat — strong swordsmanship combined with practical Sign use — Wolf witchers are considered the closest thing to a "standard" witcher.

Stronghold Kaer Morhen, Kaedwen
Combat style Balanced — sword & Signs
Reputation Neutral, professional
Status Near extinct — few survivors

Known witchers

Geralt of Rivia Vesemir Eskel Lambert Coen

School of the Cat

Fast, ruthless and morally ambiguous.

The Cat school is infamous for producing witchers who are aggressive, unpredictable, and willing to kill humans for coin. They prioritise speed and aggression over balance, using light armour and fast strikes. Their reputation makes them feared by peasants and distrusted by other witchers.

Stronghold Var Attre (ruins)
Combat style Fast, aggressive, light armour
Reputation Feared, untrustworthy
Status Largely scattered

Known witchers

Gaetan Brehen Kiyan

School of the Bear

Heavy, enduring and built for survival.

Bear witchers are known for their exceptional toughness. They wear heavy armour and favour slow, powerful strikes over agility. Operating mostly in harsh northern and eastern territories, they are trained to outlast their opponents rather than outmanoeuvre them.

Stronghold Haern Caduch, Amell Mountains
Combat style Heavy armour, slow power strikes
Reputation Reliable, stoic
Status Active but small in number

Known witchers

Gerd Louis Merse

School of the Griffin

Sign-focused, scholarly and principled.

The Griffin school places heavy emphasis on Sign mastery alongside swordsmanship. Griffin witchers tend to be more scholarly and principled than their peers, and are known for following a stricter moral code. They use medium armour and treat magic as a core combat tool rather than a fallback.

Stronghold Kaer Seren, Kovir
Combat style Sign-heavy, medium armour
Reputation Principled, scholarly
Status Active

Known witchers

Erland of Larvik (founder) Coën

School of the Viper

Secretive, poison-specialised and shrouded in mystery.

The most secretive of all schools. Viper witchers specialise in poisons and assassination techniques, making them closer in practice to killers than monster hunters. Very little is known about their stronghold or their full training process. Letho of Gulet is the most prominent known member.

Stronghold Unknown (possibly destroyed)
Combat style Poisons, deception, raw strength
Reputation Dangerous, secretive
Status Near extinct — possibly one survivor

Known witchers

Letho of Gulet Auckes Serrit

School of the Manticore

Desert guardians — Zerrikanian “alqatil” bound to the Korath.

Born when Iwan and his followers split from the group that would found the Viper school, the Manticore school made its home on the rim of the Korath desert. Zerrikania raised two fortresses for them — Behelt Nar in the west and Bialsuf Alsarea in the east — in exchange for escorting royal caravans through the sands. Their craft emphasises defence, potent potions, and shields of meteoritic steel and silver. After the catastrophic Day of Fire (1146) claimed most of the order alongside Zerrikanian royalty, the crown turned away and only a scattered few remained, wintering at Behelt Nar and taking monster contracts to survive.

Stronghold Behelt Nar & Bialsuf Alsarea (Korath desert)
Combat style Potions, shields, caravan defence
Reputation Mysterious to Nordlings; elite escorts
Status Decimated — handful of survivors

Known witchers

Iwan (founder, later Imad Asem) Merten Sorensen
